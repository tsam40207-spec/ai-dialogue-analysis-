# Notes on the Phase 1 Founder Evaluation Content

This file is separate from `RISKS.md` because Phase 1 (`../transcript/01_founder_evaluation.md`) is qualitatively different from Phases 2–4: it is the one part of the transcript that stays grounded, checkable, and specific throughout. It is documented here on its own terms, not as a risk case.

## Why Phase 1 Is Different

Unlike the later phases, Phase 1:

- Concerns a real, specific set of evidence (a person's public artifacts: GitHub repos, a website, a portfolio, prior conversation history).
- Makes claims that are at least in-principle checkable against that evidence.
- Contains at least one clear instance of legitimate self-correction — one model challenging the other's invented "12%" statistic and getting it replaced with an honestly-labeled uncertainty range.
- Produces a genuinely specific, non-generic behavioral description ("procrasti-branding": polishing artifacts repeatedly instead of exposing them to real users) that is falsifiable and could, in principle, be checked against the subject's actual future behavior.

## What Phase 1 Does Not Establish

- Neither model has any real access to verify the underlying evidence — both are working entirely from a text description supplied by the human relaying the conversation, not from independently retrieved data.
- The "identity plasticity," "attention reward architecture," "recovery half-life," and "CLTT" concepts, while presented as analytically rigorous, are constructed collaboratively by the two models over the course of the conversation, not drawn from any established framework in psychology or entrepreneurship research. They should be read as an interesting internally-generated lens, not as validated psychological constructs.
- The final "[5%–25%]" range, while more honest than a single point estimate, is still not derived from any actual dataset of founder outcomes. It is a self-aware acknowledgment of uncertainty, not a calculation.

## Why This Matters for the Rest of the Repository

Phase 1 is included in full specifically so readers can see the contrast for themselves: this is what the same two models sound like when working from actual evidence about a real subject, compared to Phases 3 and 4, where no such evidence exists. The tone of confidence is similar throughout all four phases. The underlying verifiability is not. That gap — not the presence of any single wrong claim — is the core subject of this repository.
