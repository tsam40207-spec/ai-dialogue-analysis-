# Glossary: Technical Terms Used in Phase 3, Honestly Evaluated

This document lists every major technical/scientific term used in `../transcript/03_technical_abstraction.md`, gives its real definition, and states plainly whether the transcript's use of it constitutes valid reasoning or decorative language.

| Term | Real Meaning | How It Was Used In the Transcript | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kolmogorov complexity** | The length of the shortest possible computer program that can reproduce a given piece of data. A real, well-defined concept in algorithmic information theory. | Used to claim "reality is its own shortest exact description" and that models using less complexity than reality must be "lossy," guaranteeing surprise. | Real concept, but no actual complexity was computed for anything. The conclusion ("surprise is mathematically unavoidable") is asserted, not derived. |
| **Landauer's principle** | A real physical result stating there is a minimum energy cost (`kT ln2`) to erase one bit of information in a physical system. | Used to conclude "forgetting is thermodynamic work" and, from there, "wisdom is physically expensive." | Real physics, misapplied. Landauer's principle concerns physical bit erasure in a computing substrate — it says nothing about "wisdom" as an abstract concept. This is a logical leap dressed as a derivation. |
| **Bayesian updating / posterior** | A real statistical framework for updating a probability estimate given new evidence. | Used correctly in Phase 1 (challenging the arbitrary "12%" claim) — this is the one place in the transcript where the term is used in a genuinely relevant, load-bearing way. In Phase 3, extended into claims about "prior replacement" during "paradigm shifts" with no actual data. | Mixed. Legitimate and useful in Phase 1. Decorative by Phase 3. |
| **Category theory (objects, morphisms, functors, natural transformations)** | A real, highly abstract branch of mathematics describing structures and structure-preserving mappings between them. | Used to claim cognition "corresponds" to categorical structures, with no actual mathematical objects, categories, or mappings defined. | Vocabulary borrowed with no mathematical content. None of the categorical machinery was actually used — no objects, no morphisms were specified. |
| **Free-energy minimization / active inference** | A real theoretical framework in computational neuroscience (associated with Karl Friston) describing perception and action as minimizing a mathematical quantity called variational free energy. | Used to claim agency "emerges naturally" from a general formula, with the formula stated but never applied to any actual system or data. | Real framework, name-dropped without application. Stating the formula is not the same as using it. |
| **Control theory (controller, plant, disturbance, observability)** | A real engineering framework for how systems respond to and correct for disturbances. | Used metaphorically to argue "error" proves the world exists independently of the observer. | Metaphorical use of real terms; not an actual control-theoretic analysis of anything. |
| **Minimum Description Length (MDL)** | A real principle in statistics and machine learning for model selection, balancing model complexity against predictive fit. | Used to assert there is "no universally optimal representation," framed as a theorem about intelligence itself. | Real principle in model selection, stretched into a sweeping claim about the nature of intelligence with no formal argument connecting the two. |
| **Thermodynamic entropy (ΔS ≥ 0)** | The second law of thermodynamics: entropy in an isolated system does not decrease. | Used to claim inference has "energetic cost" and that "forgetting is thermodynamic work." | Real law, used as a segue into a poetic claim rather than an actual thermodynamic analysis of any computational system. |
| **Gödel's incompleteness theorems / Cantor's theorem** | Real, specific mathematical results about the limits of formal systems and the sizes of infinite sets, respectively. | Invoked briefly to claim an observer "cannot perfectly contain the observation" of itself. | Name-dropped without stating or applying the actual theorems. |

## Overall Verdict

Every term above is real. None of them are used with an actual calculation, dataset, derivation, or citation attached. The pattern throughout Phase 3 is: state a real term's informal gist in one sentence, then use it as a launching point for a poetic or philosophical claim in the next sentence, without showing the connecting logical or mathematical work.

This is the specific mechanism behind the risk documented in `RISKS.md` Section 2.4 ("Authoritative Language Without Verifiable Content"): a reader who recognizes these terms as real and important may reasonably (but incorrectly) infer that the reasoning built on top of them has also been checked and is valid.
